My name is Tiffany Chau. I am from Los Angeles, California. I am a first year college student studying Business Administration at the University of California, Riverside. I am still debating on what I want to concentrate in the future, but I know it would revolve around Business. If my family were to described me using three words they would say hardworking, organized, and respectful. My ultimate goal in life is to use all my skills to be successful in all aspects of my life.

I received my high school dipolma in 2020, graduating from Alhambra High School. In high school, the majority of my time spent on extracurriculars related to volunteering. Some of the organizations that I volunteered with included the American Red Cross, Academy of Future Educators, and Kaibigan Service Club. Being apart of the organizations taught me a lot about teamwork as I needed to work alongside new people in our to achieve goals. In Red Cross, I served as Red Cross President for two years volunteering at events, hosting weekly meetings, and setting up blood drives.

Some of my interests include sports, art, and baking. I enjoy watching the NBA and playing basketball with my friends and cousins. I like to work on art projects during my free time. From time to time, I would find objects around the house and draw them in my sketch pad. I also like to bake sweets with my sister.

My job experience involves tutoring students both inside and outside of school. During my junior year I joined AFE and was able to serve as a tutor for a 10th grade and 11th grade math class at my high school. Furthermore, I was able to take on a tutoring position where I tutored a 5th grade student and 11th grade student. One of the most important things that I learned from my tutoring experience is that all students learn differently. To be an effective tutor, one must be able to find alternatives strategies to help students understand concepts more efficiently.
